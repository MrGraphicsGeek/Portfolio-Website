Case Study: Infinity Spacelines Website

Infinity Spacelines was a passion project I did in my spare time to sharpen both my coding and design skills. It's a fantasy website based around travel and tourism. Since this was a fictitious project, I decided to have fun with it and base it around space, something I've always been interested in.

Project type: Web Design

Skills involved:

  • Website Design
  • Logo Design
  • Image Design

Tools/Programs Used:

  • Adobe Dreamweaver
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator

Duration:
5 months, August 2022-January 2023

The Process

Rough Sketching

I started out the process with various rough sketches of site pages and other design ideas. Since I decided Infinity Spacelines is set in a period where space travel is as common as air travel, I looked to those respective sites for inspiration when it came to the general layout. For further ideas, I visited other miscellaneous websites on the internet to see what other features I could implement.

I did photoshop drawings after sketching on paper, where I refined the look of the pages even further. I decided the site would have 5 main pages in addition to the home page. Still, I didn't concern myself with every little detail at this point, knowing I could come up with more ideas as I went.

Coding

Once I had a decent idea of how the site should look, it was time to start coding. Since I hadn't made any real images or icons yet, I knew the site was going to look like a bare skeleton. This would give me the chance to test and refine things like image dimensions without being committed to anything.

One of the elements I'm most happy with is the main header and navigation. A fixed header is something I've been meaning to add to one of my websites for a while, but never understood how. After much research on methods that involved JS, I was finally able to pull it off using pure CSS, giving me exactly what I wanted.

With the framework of Infinity Spacelines now in place, I was free to start work on the artistic side of the project.

Creating a logo

Creating a site for a fictional business meant I'd also be designing the company's logo. As I soon as I came up with the name "Infinity Spacelines", I immediately began sketching ideas. An early logo I had in mind featured the initialism "IS", but I soon realized that this looked too much like the word "is".

I'd say the most challenging aspect of this was finding the right font. I knew using something generic like Arial black would be too boring, but a more complex font would look too busy and wouldn't fit the sleek, futuristic look I was going for. Eventually I found the perfect balance in a font called Quicksilver Italic.

The final result is a logo I'm very proud of. I think it perfectly captures the space-age aesthetic I was after.

Image Design

The sites images' were done by me in Photoshop and Illustrator, mainly utilizing stock images I found on free image websites. This is where I let my imagination run wild, dreaming up things like flying buses to alien temples to food floating in zero gravity.

Since I knew I'd have a hard time finding images of things like spaceships, I had to either create them from scratch, or trace them from an existing image. For the header image seen on the home page, tracing an image of a private jet made for a sufficent starship.

In all, I made about 35 custom images for this project. It was a lot of fun implementing all these different elements into a functioning website.

The Final Product

Results

I think that the Infinity Spacelines project was a great success and makes a fine addition to my portfolio. This is the most elaborate website I've done so far. I've now gained more confidence in my abilities, and I'm looking forward to applying the skills I learned here to my future projects.
